What are the top three dental technologies that are changing the industry?

The oral care market is growing, with a global value of more than $100 billion. This growth is expected to continue, and dental professionals need to keep up with what’s new in technology to stay relevant and competitive. According to a recent study by Pew Research, 35% of adults in the U.S. used some sort of digital health service in 2018. With so many people turning to apps and websites for information about their health and well-being, it’s unsurprising that technology is playing a large role in dentistry as well. In the last few years, we’ve seen a rise in the use of AI, VR, and AR in dental practice, as well as more and more interest in 3D printing in dental fields.The dental industry is still fairly young, and as such, it’s constantly evolving and adopting new technologies and research practices. The following are some of the top innovations that are changing the way dentists perform their jobs and the services they offer their patients:

Digital X-Rays

X-rays have been used as a part of dental diagnostics for more than a century. They’re still widely used today, but they’re being modified to become more user-friendly and more sensitive to different materials. In the past few years, there have been significant advancements in digital X-ray technology.These include wireless X-ray machines that allow dentists to take images of patients in their homes, as well as smaller, more portable models that can be used in the office. There’s also a shift toward a digital file, which can be easily shared with other dental professionals and patients via email or online portals.This is good news for patients, who can take the images at home, eliminating the need to come into the office, and it’s good for dentists, who can easily access information about their patients’ teeth.

Digital 3D Printing

3D printing has been around for decades, but it’s only recently become a more common practice in dentistry. This technology has a wide variety of applications, from creating customized dental implants and crowns to designing crowns and braces that are tailored to the specific aesthetic and functional needs of patients.Dental 3D printing is still in its infancy, and many practices are using the technology as a way to prototype new solutions and test their effectiveness. However, this technology is expected to grow significantly over the next few years, with more and more dental practices implementing 3D technology.Dental 3D printers are also being used in the field of digital dentistry. This technology, which can be used to create customized 3D dental models, allows dentists to create a 3D image of a patient’s teeth and gums that can be used for a variety of dental procedures, such as crowns and implants. This is a more efficient way to design and create dental prosthetics than the traditional method of using wax molds.

Augmented Reality and Virtual Reality (VR)

VR has been used in many different industries for years, but its use in the dental field is fairly recent. However, it’s expected to become more popular in the coming years.VR has many potential benefits for the dental industry, especially for patients in remote and under-served communities who may have limited access to quality care.For example, VR allows patients to virtually walk through a dental professional’s office and explore different treatments, including dental implants and crowns. This allows patients to make informed decisions about their oral health and determine if they’re a good candidate for certain procedures.VR can also be used in dental education, allowing dental students to practice complex procedures before they’re ready to perform them on patients. This will help them to build their skills and become more comfortable with challenging procedures.

Digital Marketing and Communication

Dentists are increasingly using digital marketing to reach their patients. This includes blogging, social media, and online advertising. Some practices are also integrating video conferencing into their digital strategy.This is another example of how technology is changing the way dentists do business. It allows them to reach their patients in new and effective ways.Dental practices can use digital marketing to share information about new services, patient testimonials, and information about the various benefits of visiting a dentist. In some cases, this digital marketing is even being used to increase dental tourism; i.e., encouraging patients to travel to their local dentist in another city or state.The rise of digital marketing is also allowing practices to connect with potential patients in new ways. They’re able to reach a broader audience and promote their services to people who weren’t previously aware of their services.

Conclusion

The dental industry is always evolving, and it’s important that dental professionals keep up with the latest technologies and research. The technologies listed above are just a few of the ways that dental practice is being transformed by new research, advancements in technology, and new ways of thinking about patient care.If you want to stay on top of the latest trends and technologies in dentistry, make sure to follow the American Dental Association and sign up for their newsletter. You can also follow dental technology news sites and social media accounts to keep up to date with the latest developments and trends.
