What are the most common dental emergencies and how can they be avoided?

Dental emergencies can happen on any day or time. From losing a filling to getting a cavity filled so quickly that it becomes a nightmare for both you and your dentist, there are many things that can go wrong while you’re getting your teeth worked on. However, most dental emergencies can be dealt with, either by bringing your patient to the dentist right away or by providing instructions for self-care until they arrive at the office. If you have a dental emergency on you, it is important to know what to do in order not to harm your teeth or gums further. Here are some pointers on what are the most common dental emergencies and how you can deal with them accordingly:

Loose fillings

If you find that one or more of your fillings are loose and are getting in the way of brushing and flossing, don’t panic! You can generally make those fillings stay in place by using a variety of methods. If you’re still in the process of drilling the teeth that those fillings are in, you can take a small piece of your own dental floss and gently push the fibers between the fillings and the teeth holding them in place. You can also take a clean, dry paintbrush and lightly brush the fillings toward the back of the teeth to which they’re attached to move them slightly. As an added bonus, brushing the fillings will also freshen up the appearance of your teeth since the fillings will get a bit of a makeover if they’ve been around for a while.

Lost crowns

If you’ve lost a crown, don’t panic! You can usually find most crowns on the same table where the teeth they’re attached to were worked on. If you’re not able to find it on your own, you can always seek help from a friend or family member and have them ask if anyone has seen it. You can also search online for lost crowns. You can also try to find your crown by feeling around the teeth where it was attached. If you can’t find it, you can take a bite of food and save the piece in your mouth to see if it comes out later. You can also try to search for it with a dental explorer as it is slightly more effective than just feeling around with your fingers.

Damaged teeth

If you have a tooth that feels loose or if you notice a lot of pain, don’t panic! Depending on the severity of the situation, you can either see a dentist immediately or try to save the tooth by using a few common household items. To save a broken or chipped tooth, you can use a cotton swab and some toothpaste or baking soda. You can also try using some dental floss to tie a piece of thread around the broken part and then tie the thread to the other side of your teeth. You can also try dipping a small cotton swab in sugar or salt, placing it on the broken tooth, and then covering it with plastic wrap.If you have an aching tooth, you can try to save it by covering it with a small piece of cotton and then placing that cotton in an empty plastic water bottle. Make sure that the water bottle is the right size so that the cotton will cover your entire tooth. If you have an abscessed tooth, you can try to save it by putting some baking soda or salt in a small bowl and then covering the tooth with the bowl to create a soft, padded bandage. You can also try to save an abscessed tooth by covering it with a small piece of cloth or cotton.

Damaged gums

If you have a gums that are irritated and swollen, you may want to use a cold compress on them to help ease the pain. You can also try to save a gums that are swollen by covering them with a piece of gauze or a small bandage to keep them clean and protect them from further damage.

Toothache

If you have a toothache, you can try to save it by using a few common household items. You can try to relieve the pain by placing a piece of cotton or gauze in the space between your teeth and gums where the pain is coming from. This can help to reduce the pain and pressure on the teeth and gums. You can also try to save a toothache by placing a small piece of gauze in a small bowl of hot water and then placing it on the affected teeth.The same goes for a swollen gums. You can place a piece of gauze or cotton in the space between the teeth and gums to help reduce the swelling and pain.
