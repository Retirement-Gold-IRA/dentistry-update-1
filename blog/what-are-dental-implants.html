What are dental implants?

Dental implants are artificial teeth that are surgically placed in place of your natural teeth. Typically, the implants are made of titanium, which is biologically compatible with your body.Dental implants have been around for decades, but are still a relatively new technology. With the help of a dental implant, you can replace teeth that have been lost, bridge teeth that are in poor condition, or use them to anchor a dental restoration (e.g. a denture or a fixed bridge). These dental implants are also known as endosseous implants.

What are the benefits of dental implants?

Dental implants are a long-term restoration option that can provide many benefits to your oral health. Because they are placed deep inside your jawbone, dental implants are generally more stable than other restorative treatments, like dental bridges or crowns. This means they are less likely to be damaged by chewing, grinding, or other movements of your mouth.Dental implants also have a high success rate and long-lasting life expectancy. They can last for decades, making them a cost-effective investment for your oral health.

Types of dental implants

There are several types of dental implants: - Endosseous implants: These are the most common type of dental implants and are surgically placed into the jawbone. - Hybrid implants: These are a combination of endosseous and endohybrid (non-osseous) implants. - Endohybrid implants: These are non-osseous implants that are usually placed in the gum or teeth. - Bar-clip implants: These are mini implants that can be placed in between teeth without a surgical procedure. - Hybrid endo/bar-clip implants: These are a combination of endosseous and bar-clip implants. - Mandibular (skull) implants: These are used for extractions in the upper arch and can be placed in the upper part of the jawbone.
