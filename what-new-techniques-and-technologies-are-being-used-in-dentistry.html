What new techniques and technologies are being used in dentistry?

The way dentists examine, diagnose and treat patients is constantly evolving to match the needs of patients and the latest technologies. This is not only a reflection of the times but is also driven by a desire to use the best tools available to ensure high-quality patient care.As new technologies emerge, they often fill in gaps left by other tools. In other words, they provide an option for care providers who are looking for a more focused solution or who need something that meets a specific need.There are many examples of technologies that have become essential in dental practice. These include digital X-rays, 3-D digital imaging, Cone Beam Computed Tomography (CBCT), digital scaling, intra-oral scanners, digital articulators, etc. In this blog post, we look at some of these technologies, including their benefits, downsides and how they fit into the bigger picture of modern day dentistry.

Digital Radiography (DR)

Digital radiography (DR) uses digital sensors instead of X-ray films. This has several advantages, including increased speed, reduced costs and better image quality.DR is a form of digital X-ray that uses computer software to process and then display digital images of the patient’s teeth. This can allow the dentist to see a large amount of data in one image. This can help to improve efficiency and accuracy when conducting exams, especially in pediatric and special-needs patients.

3-D Imaging

3-D imaging is another technology that is quickly becoming essential in dental practice. 3-D imaging technologies use different technologies to capture images of a patient’s teeth, jaws and surrounding tissues. This allows dentists to view a 3-D model of a patient’s teeth, surrounding tissues and bite chart. This allows them to plan and perform complex procedures with greater accuracy and confidence. 3-D imaging is also useful for patient education and marketing. This technology can be used to produce detailed models of teeth and bite charts, which can be shared with patients via websites and apps.

Computer-Aided Design (CAD)

CAD is a software program that allows dentists to create 3-D models of teeth and bite charts. This allows them to plan and perform complex procedures with greater accuracy and confidence. This technology can be used to create a 3-D model of each patient’s teeth so that they can be virtually placed in an ideal bite position.Computer-Aided Manufacturing (CAM) is another technology that is often used in combination with CAD. This allows dentists to create physical models of teeth, bite charts and other dental appliances. This technology can be used to create custom oral appliances, crowns and other dental prostheses.Computer Assisted Surgical (CAS) is a system that combines CAD and CAM technologies to help surgeons perform more precise and complex surgeries. It allows surgeons to plan surgeries in 3-D and then use computer-guidance to perform them.

Digital Scanners

Digital Scanners are devices that allow dentists to digitize their patient’s teeth using a 3-D model. This allows them to create an accurate 3-D model of the teeth and surrounding tissues. This can be used for diagnosis and treatment planning, as well as for digital marketing. This technology can be used in combination with 3-D imaging technologies, allowing dentists to create digital models of teeth and bite charts.

Digital Articulators

Digital Articulators are devices that allow dentists to simulate the movements of the jaws in a 3-D model. This allows them to plan and perform complex treatments that replicate the natural movements of the teeth and jaws. This technology can be used to plan complex treatments that replicate the movements of the teeth and jaws. It can also be used to plan treatments based on specific goals. This technology can be used to plan complex treatments that replicate the movements of the teeth and jaws. It can also be used to plan treatments based on specific goals.

Computer-Assisted Teeth Whitening

Computer-Assisted Teeth Whitening (CATTRO) is a teeth whitening system that allows patients to whiten their teeth from the comfort of their own home. This system uses light-emitting diodes (LEDs) to accelerate teeth whitening. It combines a special mouthguard with a computer-controlled laser system to accelerate teeth whitening through the use of light.CATTRO uses an active diode-laser system that emits light into the teeth from the inside, accelerating the process of teeth whitening. The light causes a biocidal reaction that bleaches teeth by accelerating the production of reactive oxygen species (ROS) inside the teeth.CATTRO uses a special mouthguard that is placed in the patient’s mouth. This device controls the dosage of laser light and also protects the teeth from overexposure. It can be used to accelerate teeth whitening.

Conclusion

Dental technologies are evolving rapidly and with that comes a need for constant education and training. While it might seem like an intimidating prospect, the benefits of staying informed and keeping up with the latest technologies are immeasurable.
